*   **Status:** ❌ Unsolved

*   **Symptoms:** 
*   **What I've Tried:** Using the On and Kun readings of the kanji to match the readings. However, due to Japanese linguistic features such as 連濁, 音便, and 熟字訓, the On and Kun readings method would not be very accurate. 
*   **Notes:** Here is a list of the four 音便 types:
1. I-onbin (イ音便): A euphonic change to an "i" sound. Example: 書く (kaku) → かきて (kakite) → かいて (kaite)
2. Sokuonbin (促音便): A euphonic change to a geminate consonant (the small "っ"). Example: 立つ (tatsu) → たつて (tatsute) → たって (tatte)
3. Hatsuonbin (撥音便): A euphonic change to an "n (ん)" sound. Example: 読む (yomu) → よみて (yomite) → よんで (yonde)
4. U-onbin (ウ音便): A euphonic change to a "u" sound (mostly seen in dialects or older Japanese, such as ありがとう (arigatou) evolving from ありがたく (arigataku))
